Constitution for the
IEEE - CS Student Branch Chapter at USF
Founding Date of the Organization: May 27, 2021
Date Constitution Last Amended: April 23, 2024
Date Constitution Last Reviewed: April 23, 2024
Article I. NAME
The name of this student organization shall be the Institute of Electrical and Electronics
Engineers – Computer Society Student Branch Chapter at the University of South Florida
hereafter referred to as any of the following:
A. IEEE – Computer Society Student Branch Chapter at the University of South Florida
B. IEEE – Computer Society Student Branch Chapter at USF
C. IEEE–CS Student Branch Chapter at the University of South Florida
D. IEEE–CS Student Branch Chapter at USF
E. IEEE – Computer Society at the University of South Florida
F. IEEE – Computer Society at USF
G. IEEE–CS at the University of South Florida
H. IEEE–CS at USF
I. IEEE – Computer Society
J. IEEE–CS
K. Or any other alternative variation of the organization’s name and equivalent
abbreviation, including but not limited to replacing “at” with “@” or removing “–” in
such name or variation.
Article II. PURPOSE, GOALS, and AFFILIATION
The purpose and goal of this student organization are to bring together students at the
University of South Florida (USF) to learn, network, collaborate, and share ideas
regarding the theory and practice of all aspects of computer science and engineering,
while promoting professional development through student-led talks and outreach
activities.
This organization is affiliated with the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers –
Computer Society (IEEE–CS).
This organization is associated with the USF Department of Computer Science and
Engineering (CSE).
Article III. MEMBERSHIP QUALIFICATIONS
A. Requirement for Membership
Only undergraduate and graduate students currently enrolled in the University of South
Florida – all campuses are eligible for membership. 100% of active membership must be
USF students and open to all students. Associate members may be USF faculty, staff, or
alumni and spouses and domestic partners of student members. These associate members
shall not be given the rights and privileges of active USF students including but not
limited to voting rights, use of facilities, or equipment, and/or benefits from studentfunded resources.
B. Length of Term for membership
Membership will take effect when an interested party confirms membership on the
organization roster. Active membership continues for one (1) academic school year or
until an individual has been removed by the organization. Associative membership
continues until either the individual withdraws or has been removed by the organization.
C. Requirements for continued membership
Members are required to uphold the objectives and purpose of the organization, and to
comply with University policies. They must also be currently enrolled students. These are
the only requirements for membership.
D. Specifications for the removal of members from the group
If any member fails to uphold the purpose and objectives of the organization, does not
meet membership requirements, or does not comply with University policies, he/she may
be held accountable and in jeopardy of losing their membership. Reason for removal of
member must be presented to the Executive Board. Written notification shall then be
presented with reason for removal to the member in question. The member in question
has the right to speak to the board on their own behalf. Then, a vote is taken and he/she
may be removed by a 2/3’s vote of the Executive board.
E. Voting Rights
Only Active USF students in the organization have voting rights. Non-USF students do
not have voting rights.
Article IV. DUES/FEES
No local dues, fees, assessments, donations, or other charges are levied for membership.
Article V. QUORUM
A quorum shall be composed of 50% + 1 of current, active voting members. Quorum
must be present for any vote to take place on organization issues or amendments to this
constitution.
Article VI. OFFICERS
A. Officer Positions
The required officer positions include: President, Vice President, and Treasurer. These
officers are elected to serve a one (1) academic school year term. These positions are
included in the Executive Board. Optional officer positions may be created by a majority
vote of the Executive Board to serve the organization. Optional officers are elected to
serve a one (1) academic school year term. The duties and titles of these offices will be
created and established in the organizational bylaws. Only current USF student members
can hold office.
B. Main Officers’ Duties
An Executive Board comprised of Officers will govern the activities of the organization,
and the specific duties of the required Officers will be as follows:
1. President: shall preside over meetings; oversee organizational functions; act as a
primary University liaison; and conduct decisions as established in the organizational
bylaw.
2. Vice President: shall assist the President with their duties and preside over the
organization in the President's absence.
3. Treasurer: shall receive, dispatch, and keep accurate records of all financial matters
regarding the organization. Furthermore, the Treasurer shall submit the organization's
annual/interim budget forms, purchase orders, or related to Student Government or ECouncil for A&S financial allocation and IEEE-FWCS or IEEE-CS for additional
financial allocation from the main affiliated organization.
4. Secretary (Optional): shall keep a record of all correspondences, meetings, club’s
bylaws, activities, and other organizational documents; inform officers of deadlines for
reports, mailings, and future commitments; maintain a roster of officers and members
with their current contact information; submit or manage event attendance report in both
BullsConnect and IEEE vTools. Works closely with Event Chair, Webmaster, Social
Media Chair, Graphic Design Chair, Collaboration/Outreach Chair, and TC Chairs.
5. Event Chair (Optional): shall collaborate with other officers to create and execute
exciting, interesting events for the club members; oversee, coordinate, and collect reports
about all events organized by the TC Chairs, Logistics Chair, Collaboration/Outreach
Chair, or other officers; communicates with the Social Media Chair and Graphics Design
Chair for the creation of marketing publications for events; and maintain a list of all
events and statistics to be reported to the Executive Board and advisor. The Event Chair
may also be able to propose the creation or removal of TCs for new recurring events as
needed, with respective approval from the President or Executive Board as established in
Article VI, D. Works closely with the Secretary, Social Media Chair, Graphics Design
Chair, and Webmaster to promote upcoming events to all members accordingly.
6. Webmaster (Optional): shall develop and maintain club website and social media sites;
find ways to use technology to improve the organization's operations (e.g.,
communications, outreach, task flow, planning, logistics, organizing); and inform other
officers and members about technological resources available to the organization. Works
closely with the Secretary, Event Chair, and Communications Chair.
7. Social Media Chair (Optional): shall manage all the club social media and posting
platforms. This chair shall publicize all club activities through the club’s email, social
media, BullsConnect, website, and Microsoft Teams channel with approval from the
President. Must be present on most of events or find another officer as a substitute to
attend an event and create content for the club social media. Works closely with the
Secretary to maintain records of correspondence; Event Chair and TC Chairs to promote
upcoming events to all members, accordingly; and Graphic Design Chair to create any
flyers and digital designs as necessary.
8. Graphic Design Chair (Optional): shall create all the club’s digital graphic designs as
necessary, such as flyers and artworks for promotional item. Furthermore, this chair shall
review and advise on any graphical designs created by other officers to USF and IEEECS marketing rules, such as posters, banners, handouts, logos, and more. This chair shall
be familiar or experienced with using Adobe Creative Cloud software for graphic designs
(e.g., Adobe Illustrator, InDesign) and all the marketing/branding guidelines for both
USF and IEEE-CS. All designs must be approved by the President, the event organizer (if
any), and/or majority vote by the Executive Board. Works closely with Social Media
Chair, Event Chair, and/or TC Chairs to get the necessary details for all club flyers; and
Secretary to maintain records of all documentations and designs.
9. Collaboration/Outreach Chair (Optional): shall find and contact companies, industries,
organizations, and professional and academic experts in CSE-related topics for
collaboration, such as guest speakers, practical experience for students, tours,
networking, project mentorship, professional development, fundraising, and more.
Furthermore, this chair shall serve as a primary contact for the organization and with
IEEE Florida West Coast Section (FWCS), IEEE Computer Society (CS), and IEEE main
organizations. This chair shall attend every IEEE-FWCS ExCom monthly meeting to
provide IEEE-FWCS logistic updates from our student organization. Works closely with
President, Event Chair, Treasurer, and Secretary.
10. Logistics Chair (Optional): shall manage and allocate all supplies or items purchased
by the club or for any club’s activities, such as electronic devices, kits, tools, food,
drinks, and more. This chair shall place the respective delivery request (by phone or
email) for events following respective purchasing regulation advised by Treasurer. Works
closely with the Secretary, Event Chair, and Treasurer to guarantee the adequate supplies
management and documentation.
C. All USF students who are registered members of the organization must be
allowed to run for an E-Board/Officer position regardless of college/major.
D. Technical Committees (TCs)
Technical committees (TCs) may be created as necessary by the President or majority
vote from the Executive Board for specific events and projects not to last longer than the
current officer term. Each committee will have a chair, as appointed by the President.
Any member or officer may be selected as a TC chair. Any number of members may be
on a committee.
E. Technical Committees (TC) Chairs
Technical committee (TC) Chairs are additional, optional officers (also members of the
Executive Board) that will govern specific events and projects of the organization. The
TC Chairs share similar duties and policies to the Event Chair (see Article VI, B-5) but
their executive privileges are constrained to their specific TC, and they may be appointed
by the President or Executive Board without requiring regular elections. Some TC Chairs
have specific topics focus for which they shall have sufficient experience to design, lead,
and execute the committee’s events. To “lead” an event refers to the individual(s) that
performs the tech talk, training, tutorial, presentation, project mentorship, guidance
through the practical workshop, slides/activities set-up, or the respective
production/implementation of the event. All events conducted by the TC Chairs shall
report to and get approval from the Event Chair on all activities, updates, changes, and
exciting new ideas. All TC Chairs work closely with the Treasurer for event funding as
needed; Communication Chair for promoting upcoming events to all members; Secretary
for keeping records of all documents and reminders; and Webmaster for technological
assistance. All TC Chairs have an equal ranking level but are below all Main Officers
listed in Article VI, B.
F. List of Technical Committees
An effective list of TC shall be provided under Amendment A1, with respective
descriptions. Amendment A1 shall be reviewed, updated, and approved for each
academic year by majority vote from the Executive Board of the previous year. The
approval must be done before officers’ election cycle for the respective year and provided
to the students during the nomination application process. Amendment A1 may be
updated throughout the current or future organization years by majority vote from the
current Executive Board. These updates may include: (i) add new TC, (ii) remove TC, or
(iii) modify TC descriptions.
Article VII. ELECTION OF OFFICERS
A. The elections for officers for the forthcoming year will take place in April. Any
student member may campaign or be nominated for any position on the Executive Board.
B. At least one (1) officer shall be a student in the USF CSE Department. The other
officers may be from any USF department or college. If after the elections no CSE
student was elected for any position, then the Executive Board must create a new interim
officer position and appoint a CSE student by majority vote. This new interim officer
position will have duties determined by the Executive Board and shall be terminated as
soon as any other officer position is appointed with a CSE student. This requirement can
be waived if no CSE student campaigned for any position on the Executive Board. All
USF students who are members of the organization must be allowed to run for an EBoard/Officer position regardless of college/major.
C. The President must notify members of the election timeline and nomination process at
least two weeks prior to the election nomination deadline. Nominations must be sent to
the appointed officer before the set deadline as specified by the President.
D. Nominees shall be given time to present themselves during the election meeting.
Members of the organization may ask candidates relevant questions to their position.
Discussion may occur after the questioning period. Voting shall commence afterward.
E. Elections will be conducted by ballot. The candidate for each office receiving a
majority vote of members present at the elections meeting will be considered the victor.
If no candidate receives a majority vote, then there will be a run-off between the two
candidates with the most votes. The candidate who receives the most votes will be the
victor. If a tie remains, the highest-ranking outgoing officer not involved in the run-off
will make the final decision. No member can be elected to more than one position on the
Executive Board at one time.
F. New officers take office at the last meeting of the semester in which the transition
takes place (see Article X, C) and remain in office until the next election’s officer
transition meeting.
G. If a position remains unfilled after the elections process, the newly elected President
will appoint a member to the position or re-assign duties to another officer.
H. In the event that a required officer position becomes vacant, an election to fill that
position will automatically occur. In the event the President’s position is vacated, the
Vice President takes on the interim responsibilities of the President until the next election
cycle. All other vacant officer positions will be nominated by the membership and voted
on in accordance with Article VII. In this case, the victor will take office immediately
following the election and will fulfill the term of the officer that is being replaced.
Article VIII. REMOVAL OF OFFICERS
A. If an officer fails to maintain enrollment in the University, they shall resign and/or be
removed from office immediately.
B. If it is believed that an officer fails to uphold the purpose and objectives of the
organization, to fulfill the duties of the office, to meet membership requirements, or
comply with University policies, he or she may be removed from office. Any member of
the organization may exercise the options in this article for removal of officers. One
option is a petition signed by at least half of the voting membership or Executive Board
requesting his or her removal. The petition must be verified for validity by the Executive
Board if signed by the voting membership or the organization advisor if signed by the
Executive Board. A second option is to bring forth a vote of ‘no confidence’ and written
rationale to the Executive Board. A copy of the ‘no confidence’ written rationale must be
submitted to and verified by the organization advisor. Either option must be presented at
a general meeting or Executive Board meeting depending on the origin. The officer in
question must receive at least two written notifications. Then, another meeting shall be
held at which the grievance will be presented, with both sides having the opportunity to
present a case.
C. The Advisor, if appropriate, shall preside over the removal hearing. After all
arguments have been heard, a secret-ballot vote shall be heard. If at least two-thirds of the
voting members present vote for removal, the officer shall be removed immediately.
D. Removed officer positions will be filled in accordance with Article VII, H.
Article IX. ADVISOR(S)
A. A full-time USF faculty or staff member will serve as an advisor. A member from the
USF CSE Department is preferred as the Advisor to better support the organization’s
purpose and goal, although this is not required.
B. Nominations for Advisor will take place within the Executive Board. An Advisor will
be chosen within two weeks of a vacancy. The Executive Board will choose the Advisor
by a majority vote and invite him/her to serve as Advisor for the academic year.
C. During the officers transition meeting (see Article X, C), the organization will vote on
whether to continue the Advisor appointment or not. The vote must be a majority of those
voting in order to retain the Advisor for the next academic year.
D. The duties of the Advisor include: meeting with organization officers, reviewing the
yearly budget, signing all required paperwork, and advising on issues of risk
management, organization leadership, and USF policy.
E. The Advisor can be removed for not carrying out the duties and expectations as
defined in this document. Any member can bring concerns to the Executive Board. The
Executive Board will meet with the Advisor to discuss the concerns. After this meeting,
the Executive Board will vote on whether to remove the Advisor. If there is a majority
vote, then the Advisor will be removed.
F. If an Advisor steps down, is removed, or is not re-appointed, the Executive Board will
follow the process stated in Article IX, B.
Article X. MEETINGS
A. The President and Executive Board shall determine the time and place of General
meetings by majority vote. The Executive Board shall determine the frequency, time, and
place to meet for Executive Board meetings by majority vote. Members shall be notified
with reasonable notice of the time and place before a meeting occurs.
B. Special meetings may be called upon by any Executive Board member and require at
least 48-hour notice to all members.
C. The officer transitions meeting will be held within 2 weeks following the election of
new officers.
Article XI. RULES OF ORDER
Meetings shall be run according to Robert’s Rules of Order. Robert’s Rules of Order will
be used in instances not covered in this constitution.
Article XII. AMENDMENTS
A. Amendments must be submitted to, and are subject to review and approval by, the
respective campus office responsible for student organizations for where the organization
will primarily function.
B. Written notification of the proposed amendment to all voting members must be made
by mail or email, at least two weeks in advance of any vote on changes in the
constitution.
C. A two-thirds vote of members present will be required for adoption.
D. Amendments are subject to the approval of the Center for Leadership and Civic
Engagement.
Article XIII. ANTI-HAZING CLAUSE
“This organization prohibits its members, both individually and collectively from
committing any acts of hazing as defined herein:
“Hazing" as defined by §1006.63, Florida Statutes, means any action or situation that
recklessly or intentionally endangers the mental or physical health or safety of a student
for purposes including, but not limited to, initiation or admission into or affiliation with
any organization operating under the sanction of a postsecondary institution, regardless of
a person’s willingness to participate. "Hazing" includes, but is not limited to, pressuring
or coercing the student into violating state or federal law; any brutality of a physical
nature, such as whipping, beating, branding, exposure to the elements, forced
consumption of any food, liquor, drug, or other substance; or other forced physical
activity that could adversely affect the physical health or safety of the student; and also
includes any activity that would subject the student to extreme mental stress, such as
sleep deprivation, forced exclusion from social contact, forced conduct that could result
in extreme embarrassment, or other forced activity that could adversely affect the mental
health or dignity of the student. Hazing does not include customary athletic events or
other similar contests or competitions or any activity or conduct that furthers a legal and
legitimate objective.
In addition to Florida Statutes §1006.63, hazing as defined by the USF system also
includes, but is not limited to, the forced use of alcohol; morally degrading or humiliating
games and activities; physical and psychological shocks; deception; verbal abuse;
personal servitude; kidnapping; deprivation of privileges granted to others in the
organization by use of force or duress; and any other activities which are contrary to
academic achievement, the stated purpose of the local and/or (inter)national organization,
and/or the mission, policies or regulations of the USF system or applicable state law.”

Article A1-I. INTRODUCTION
This amendment provides the list of technical committees (TC) effective for the 2023-
2024 academic year. This amendment shall be reviewed, updated, and approved for every
academic year as established in the organizational bylaw.
Article A1-II. LIST OF TECHNICAL COMMITEES
Following is the list of technical committees:
1. TC – Hardwathon: the Hardwathon (i.e., Hardware Hackathon) is a series of practical
events that provides students with training, tutorial, and hands-on challenges in hardwarerelated topics towards a competitive final activity with potential awards (depending on
available funds). Such topics include but are not limited to: (i) FPGA Design, (ii)
Hardware Security, (iii) Verilog/VHDL, (iv) ASIC Design, (v) computer Logic Design,
(vi) Computer System Design, or any other hardware related topics. All Hardwathon
activities and challenges shall be adequate for all undergraduate and graduate students, at
least for those interested in CSE-related topics. The competitive final activity shall be
determined in advance and announced with sufficient time from the submission deadline
or activity event date for the respective challenge, but no less than two weeks. The TC
Hardwathon Chair shall coordinate and execute all Hardwathon events. Other officers or
external collaboration may execute or lead the Hardwathon events in the absence of the
TC Hardwathon Chair.
2. TC – Demystifying Hardware: the Demystifying Hardware is a series of tech talks and
workshops on computer hardware-related topics. All Demystifying Hardware talks and
workshops shall be adequate for all undergraduate and graduate students, at least for
those interested in hardware or CSE-related topics. The TC Demystifying Hardware
Chair shall coordinate, lead, and execute all Demystifying Hardware events. Other
officers or external collaboration may execute or lead the Demystifying Hardware events
in the absence of the TC Demystifying Hardware Chair.
3. TC – Demystifying Software: the Demystifying Software is a series of tech talks and
workshops on software-related topics. All Demystifying Software talks and workshops
shall be adequate for all undergraduate and graduate students, at least for those interested
in software or CSE-related topics. The TC Demystifying Software Chair shall coordinate,
lead, and execute all Demystifying Software events. Other officers or external
collaboration may execute or lead the Demystifying Software events in the absence of the
TC Demystifying Software Chair.
4. TC – Professional Development: the Professional Development is a series of talks and
workshops on professional development, career advice, resume/CV building, and
interview preparation. All Professional Development talks and workshops shall be
adequate for all undergraduate and graduate students, especially for those interested in
CSE-related careers. The TC Professional Development Chair shall coordinate, lead, and
execute all Professional Development events; and works closely with Collaboration
Outreach Chair to contact other companies, industries, organizations, professional, or
academic experts to provide additional professional development and networking for the
students. Other officers or external collaboration may execute or lead the Professional
Development events in the absence of the TC Professional Development Chair.
5. TC – Mentorship: Project Mentorship is a series of workshops to allow students to
share ideas and mentor them towards designing/building their projects. These projects
will be displayed at the Engineering Expo if adequate and within the organization’s
topics focus (i.e., computer hardware and other CSE-related topics). The TC Mentorship
Chair shall coordinate, lead, and execute all Project Mentorship event series; and works
closely with Collaboration Outreach Chair to contact other companies, industries,
organizations, professional, or academic experts to provide additional project/career
mentorship for the students. Other officers or external collaboration may execute or lead
the mentorship events in the absence of the TC Mentorship.
6. TC – Fundraising: shall identify potential fundraising opportunities and develop
relationships with external organizations, companies, or individuals who may be
interested in financially supporting the organization. This may involve but not limited to:
searching and applying for grants, organizing crowdfunding campaigns, or seeking
sponsorship from local businesses or alumni networks. This chair shall be responsible for
coordinating and executing all fundraising events within the organization. Other officers
or external collaboration may execute or lead the fundraising events in the absence of the
TC Fundraiser Chair.
7. TC – Tabling: shall be responsible for executing all tabling events, which serve as an
opportunity for the organization to showcase its mission, goals, and activities to the
broader campus community. This position plays a critical role in increasing the visibility
and awareness of the organization among students, faculty, and staff. Must represent the
club on events such as: Week of Welcome (WoW), Engineering EXPO, tabling at
conferences, Bulls-Market, and more. In addition, the Tabling Co-Chair/s is/are
responsible for recruiting volunteers to assist with big tabling events such as EXPO and
conferences. This may involve but not limited to: coordinating schedules, assigning tasks,
and ensuring that volunteers have the necessary resources and information to represent
the organization effectively. Other officers and student members may collaborate or
volunteer in all tabling, as determined by the TC Tabling Co-Chair/s.
